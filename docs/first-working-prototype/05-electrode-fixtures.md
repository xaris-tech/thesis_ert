# Electrode Fixtures

## Tree and Banana Fixture

Use a reusable adjustable 25 mm nylon strap with twelve movable PVC holders.
Make holders from 25 x 25 mm PVC electrical cable trunking:

1. Cut twelve sections, each 30-40 mm long.
2. Drill a guide hole sized for the selected nail or screw.
3. Cut two horizontal strap slots.
4. Round every sharp edge.
5. Label holders E1-E12.
6. Slide them onto the strap and add the buckle.

Example material searches:

- [Shopee PVC cable trunking](https://shopee.ph/search?keyword=25x25mm%20PVC%20cable%20trunking)
- [Lazada PVC cable trunking](https://www.lazada.com.ph/catalog/?q=25x25mm%20PVC%20cable%20trunking)

## Installation

- Measure circumference at the scan plane.
- Set center-to-center spacing to circumference divided by 12.
- Install E1-E12 clockwise when viewed from above.
- Keep all electrode tips in one plane.
- Use the same electrode material, diameter, and insertion depth.
- Begin with approximately 10-15 mm insertion, adjusted for bark thickness.
- Attach numbered, equal-length alligator leads with strain relief.
- Photograph the fixture and mark the orientation of E1.

## Saline Tank Fixture

Use a rigid circular nonconductive tank with twelve identical stainless
electrodes fixed every 30 degrees. Keep electrode area and immersion depth
equal. Do not use the flexible tree strap as the final tank geometry.

## Geometry Metadata

Record circumference, estimated diameter, individual electrode positions,
insertion depth, material, scan height, and E1 orientation. Begin with a
normalized circular PyEIT mesh; measured irregular geometry is a later
improvement.
